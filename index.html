Cardiovascular diseases in individuals by categories

Cardiovascular diseases (CVDs) are the number 1 cause of death globally, taking an estimated 17.9 million lives each year, which accounts for 31% of all deaths worldwide. Four out of 5CVD deaths are due to heart attacks and strokes, and one-third of these deaths occur prematurely in people under 70 years of age.

Heart disease describes a range of conditions that affect your heart.

1. Heart diseases include: Blood vessel disease, such as coronary artery disease
2. Heart rhythm problems (arrhythmias)
3. Heart defects you're born with (congenital heart defects)
4. Heart valve disease
5. Disease of the heart muscle 6. Heart infection

High blood pressure, high blood cholesterol, and smoking are key risk factors for heart disease. Several other medical conditions and lifestyle choices can also put people at a higher risk for heart disease, including:

1. Diabetes
2. Overweight and obesity
3. Unhealthy diet
4. Physical inactivity
5. Excessive alcohol use

Experts often rely on BMI to determine if a person is overweight. The BMI estimates your level of body fat based on your height and weight.

1. Low weight - if BMI is less than 18.5
2. Normal weight - if BMI is 18.5 to 24.9
3. Overweight Class 1 - if BMI is 25.0 to 27.9
4. Overweight Class 2 - if BMI is 28.0 to 29.9
5. Class 1 - Low risk Obesity - if BMI is 30.0 to 34.9
6. Class 2 - Moderate risk Obesity - if BMI is 35.0 to 39.9
6. Class 3 - High risk Obesity - if BMI is equal to or greater than 40.0
